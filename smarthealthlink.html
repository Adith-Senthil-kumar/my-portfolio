The problem

Most wearable health platforms are built around a single person. They track your vitals, but they don't see your family — and chronic illness rarely respects that boundary.

  • Monitoring is individual-centric — no shared ecosystem for parents, children, and dependents.
  • Hereditary risk for diabetes, hypertension, and cardiovascular disease is ignored, so prevention starts too late.
  • There's no automated alerting to caregivers when a vital crosses a dangerous threshold, delaying emergency response.

What we built

SmartHealthLink turns individual tracking into a secure family health ecosystem, layered with AI risk prediction and real-time alerts.

Real-time vitals

Heart rate, SpO₂, sleep, and activity streamed from wearables via Google Health Connect into a live dashboard.

Family health view

Consent-based multi-profile monitoring of parents, children, and dependents — with warnings when anyone trends abnormal.

AI hereditary risk

A stacked-ensemble model predicts calibrated cardiovascular & metabolic risk from vitals + family history.

Automated alerts

Firebase Cloud Messaging pushes instant alerts to caregivers the moment a threshold is breached.

ABHA integration

Pulls historical records, prescriptions, and lab reports from India's ABHA digital health repository.

Rehab assistant

Interactive questionnaires track recovery progress and feed back into the analysis loop.

Architecture

A hybrid cloud pipeline: wearables are the data source, a Flutter app is the client, and Firebase + a Python ML service do the heavy lifting.

ClientFlutter + Dart — one codebase for Android & iOS, graphical dashboards and colour-coded alerts.
Data bridgeGoogle Health Connect API — normalises heart rate, SpO₂, steps & sleep across wearable brands.
BackendFirebase — Auth (email + Google), Firestore, Cloud Functions for threshold alerts, Storage, and FCM.
SecurityHTTPS + Firebase rules + role-based access (RBAC); sensitive docs locked to authorised family members.

The model

Hereditary risk is a noisy, imbalanced problem — a single classifier overfits. We used a stacked ensemble to trade variance for calibrated reliability.

  • Base learners: multiple gradient-boosted decision trees capture non-linear interactions in the health data.
  • Meta-learner: a logistic-regression classifier combines them, with a probability-calibration layer for trustworthy risk scores.
  • Data: 1,500 samples (heart rate, SpO₂, sleep, activity, age, BMI, family-disease history), 80/20 split, weighted loss to fight class imbalance.
  • Result: 90.2% accuracy / 0.924 AUC-ROC at 12.4 ms inference — beating SVM (82.5%) and a standalone GBDT (88.9%).

Outcome

An end-to-end system that merges wearable data, family health, and AI analytics in real time — and was peer-reviewed and published at IEEE I3CTCON 2026.
