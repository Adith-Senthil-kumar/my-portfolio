The problem

Small PG and hostel owners run their whole business across notebooks, spreadsheets, and WhatsApp — rent in one place, payroll in another, repairs on a sticky note.

  • Rent, dues, and deposits are tracked by hand, so collections and arrears are easy to lose.
  • Staff attendance and payroll get reconciled manually, every month.
  • Walk-in guests, expenses, and maintenance live in separate places, with no single source of truth.

What I built

StayTrack consolidates the entire property into one real-time app — seven modules, from rent to payroll to repairs, with dues tracking and printable receipts.

Rooms & occupancy

A floor-by-floor building view of every room with live occupancy, collection, dues, and availability — plus status and floor filters.

Tenants & rent

Add, edit, vacate, and delete tenants; capture KYC scans; auto-generate monthly dues; record payments; and print rent receipts.

Short-stay bookings

Day-by-day walk-in room booking with ID capture, a live balance, checkout, guest history, and printable receipts.

Staff & payroll

Roster, daily attendance, a weekly duty schedule, leave requests, and per-day-prorated payroll with printable payslips.

Expenses & maintenance

A categorized expense ledger plus a ticket board (open / in-progress / done); resolving a ticket auto-logs a repair expense.

Safety & polish

Confirmation dialogs for destructive actions, occupied-room edit guards, a light/dark theme, and responsive desktop-table ↔ mobile-card layouts.

How it works

An owner-scoped Firestore data model with security rules that lock every read and write to the signed-in owner. Real-time listeners stream data into the UI; a pure, unit-tested domain layer does the math.

Owner sign-in Owner-scoped Firestore Security rules Real-time listeners Zustand state Pure domain layer
ClientReact Native + Expo (SDK 56), TypeScript, Expo Router. NativeWind v4 (Tailwind for RN); responsive desktop-table ↔ mobile-card layouts with a light/dark theme.
Data modelOwner-scoped Firestore — users/{uid}/… subcollections for rooms, tenants, dues, staff, and more. Security rules lock every read/write to the signed-in owner.
Real-timeonSnapshot listeners wrapped in custom hooks stream live data into Zustand state, so the dashboard is always current.
DomainA pure, unit-tested layer computes occupancy, dues, and payroll. Money is stored as integer paise; dues use deterministic IDs (tenantId_monthKey) for idempotency.
MediaKYC scans are compressed in-app (expo-image-manipulator) and stored as base64 in a per-tenant documents subcollection.
Build & deploy44 Jest unit tests; the Expo web build deploys to Firebase Hosting — live and signed-in-ready.

Outcome

One real-time, cross-platform codebase runs an entire PG business — seven modules from rent to payroll to maintenance — on a hardened, owner-scoped Firestore backend. Not a single-feature CRUD demo: a working product you can sign into today.
